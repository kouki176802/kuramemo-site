# くらメモ LP・比較サイト制作

## Overview

Use this skill to create and improve Kuramemo-style affiliate landing pages and comparison pages.
The goal is not to make a generic blog page, but to produce a mobile-first comparison media page that turns SNS/news interest into careful product or service selection.

## Core principle

Make the reader understand these three things immediately:

1. What is being compared
2. Why it is worth checking now
3. What to confirm before buying or applying

Do not make a page that merely lists products. Every page must explain why each item matters, who it is for, who should avoid it, and what conditions must be checked.

## Before editing or creating a page

1. Identify the page type:
   - Top page / LP
   - Category page
   - Product comparison page
   - Service comparison page
   - Policy / profile / ad disclosure page
2. Read the relevant reference:
   - For visual and brand rules, read `references/brand-layout.md`
   - For page structure and content blocks, read `references/page-patterns.md`
   - For editorial, SEO, ad, and safety rules, read `references/editorial-safety.md`
3. Check existing project files before inventing new patterns:
   - `site_content/*.md`
   - `docs/BRAND_AND_EDITORIAL.md`
   - `docs/CATEGORY_POLICY.md`
   - `docs/KURARA_CHARACTER_GUIDE.md`
   - `docs/MIKERU_CHARACTER_GUIDE.md`
   - `trend_commerce/static_site.py`

## Required page qualities

- Mobile-first readability
- Strong first-view impact
- Clear comparison target
- Product/service cards with concrete reason to care
- Comparison table for service pages
- Individual explanation for each major service or product
- “向いている人” and “向かない人”
- “申込前 / 購入前の確認”
- Honest ad disclosure
- Character guidance where it improves comprehension, not decoration
- SEO title and headings that match real search intent

## Kuramemo voice

Write like a careful buyer’s guide, not a sales page.

Use:

- 話題だけで決めず
- 買う前に確認
- 自分の条件に合うか
- 向いている人
- 向かない人
- 公式条件を確認

Avoid:

- 絶対おすすめ
- 必ず安くなる
- 誰でも得
- 最安保証
- 効果を保証
- 稼げる
- 体験していないのに体験談のように書く

## Output expectations

When creating or revising content, provide:

- What changed
- Why it improves click, trust, SEO, or conversion
- Files changed
- Any remaining human tasks such as ASP approval, official price verification, or Search Console indexing
